Interacting with Feedback

2: Coupled resonances

In this example, there are two delay lines connected together. This means that the resultant tone depends on the interaction between both delay times. The x axis controls the delay time for one delay and the y axis controls the other.


This is similar to the way feedback with a vibrating string might work: the string (delay 1) has certain resonances depending on how long it is, or where a finger is placed on a fretboard. The extra delay applied to the string (delay 2) has different resonances depending on how long the delay is, for example, how far the string is from the amplifier in a guitar-feedback example.

Notes

  • Try moving from the lower left to the upper right. This keeps both delay lines more or less the same and tends to give quite a simple output: the tone rises and falls.
  • Try moving from the upper left to the lower right. As one delay time increases, the other decreases, causing lots of pitch jumps in the resultant tone.
  • The mixture of the two provides a balance of fairly controllable linear interactions, and unpredictable nonlinear interactions.
